What Are Appeals?

An appeal is a formal request to a higher court to review the decision of a lower court. It is not a retrial but rather a legal argument that errors were made during the trial process, such as the misinterpretation of the law, improper jury instructions, or the exclusion of crucial evidence. The goal of an appeal is to have the higher court correct these mistakes, which can result in a new trial, a revised sentence, or even the dismissal of charges.

Brownstone Laws and Their Impact on Appeals

Brownstone laws refer to a set of legal principles that emphasize the procedural safeguards and the rights of individuals during the appeals process. These laws focus on ensuring that the appellate courts conduct fair reviews of cases, preventing miscarriages of justice.
